About GridWatch

Why Transparency Matters

Data centers are the invisible infrastructure of the digital economy. They power AI, cloud computing, streaming, and finance — but their physical footprint has profound consequences for local communities, water supplies, power grids, and the environment. GridWatch exists to make those consequences visible to the public.

We aggregate data from public permit filings, utility records, EPA reports, SEC disclosures, and investigative journalism, then make it freely available to citizens, journalists, advocates, and policymakers.

⚡
Energy

US data centers consume ~200 billion kWh per year — ~2% of national electricity — and AI infrastructure may triple that by 2030.

💧
Water

A single hyperscale facility can consume 3–5 million gallons daily. In drought regions like the Southwest, this competes directly with agriculture and municipal needs.

🏘️
Community

Data centers create 20–80 permanent jobs per facility while placing enormous strain on local infrastructure, utilities, and housing.

⚖️
Regulation

No federal disclosure requirements exist for data center water or energy use. Most states have none either. Independent tracking is essential.

🌡️
Climate

Diesel generators, refrigerants, and grid dependency contribute to local air quality problems and greenhouse gas emissions at scale.

💰
Subsidies

Data centers routinely receive massive property tax abatements. GridWatch tracks publicly available incentive data so citizens can evaluate the tradeoffs.

Our Data Sources

Every facility in our database is linked to at least one verifiable public record. We document our methodology and welcome corrections.

Federal
EPA NPDES Water PermitsIndustrial water discharge and withdrawal permits, searchable by state and facility type. Primary source for water impact data.
Federal
EIA Forms 860 & 861US Energy Information Administration utility-scale generation and large commercial electricity consumption records.
Federal
SEC EDGAR FilingsPublic companies disclose material data center capex, acquisitions, and site details in 10-K and 8-K filings.
State
Public Utility Commission Interconnection RequestsUtility interconnection filings often reveal planned facilities months or years before public announcements. Our most valuable early-warning source.
Local
County Building & Zoning PermitsLocal permit databases capture construction, electrical, and site development activity. We monitor 200+ county databases.
Industry
Data Center Dynamics / Structure ResearchIndustry trade publications with primary reporting on facility announcements, expansions, and closures.
Community
Verified User SubmissionsCitizens, journalists, and community groups submit new facilities via our submission form. All submissions are verified before publishing.
